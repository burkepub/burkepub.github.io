The Free Models Router on OpenRouter

Categories: AI, Free Services

OpenRouter's Free Models Router (ID: openrouter/free) is a special routing service that automatically selects from available free models. No configuration needed.

How It Works

When you use openrouter/free, the router analyzes your request and picks the best available free model. It's designed to:

  • Match your request: Coding, writing, reasoning - different models excel at different things
  • Balance load: Distributes requests across providers
  • Maximize availability: If one model is busy, tries another

Technical Details

  • Context window: 200,000 tokens
  • Pricing: $0/$0 (input/output)
  • No credit card: Required

The Free Models It Uses

The router draws from about 28 free models. Top picks include:

  • Qwen 3.6 Plus: Best overall rankings, 1M context
  • Nemotron 3 Super: 120B params, NVIDIA open weights
  • GLM 4.5 Air: Has "thinking" and "non-thinking" modes
  • MiniMax M2.5: Office productivity focus

When to Use Direct Models

While the router is convenient, sometimes you want a specific model:

  • For coding: Use Qwen 3.6 Plus directly
  • For long context: Qwen 3.6 Plus (1M tokens)
  • For vision: Nemotron Nano 12B VL

My Recommendation

Start with openrouter/free to see what works for your use case. Then experiment with specific models to find what you prefer.

For my workflow (coding assistance via OpenCode), I find that specific models often perform better than the router - but your mileage may vary.
